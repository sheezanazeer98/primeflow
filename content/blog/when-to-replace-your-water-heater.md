Traditional tank water heaters typically last **8–12 years**; tankless units often last **15–20 years** with maintenance. If your unit is aging, watch for these warning signs.

## 1. Rusty or Cloudy Hot Water

Corrosion inside the tank or failing anode rod can discolor water. If only hot water is affected, the tank may be failing.

## 2. Rumbling or Popping Noises

Sediment buildup causes overheating and popping sounds. Flushing may help; severe buildup often means replacement is more cost-effective.

## 3. Moisture or Puddles Around the Base

Small leaks often worsen quickly. Water pooling under the tank usually means the inner tank has failed — repair is rarely practical.

## 4. Inadequate Hot Water

If your family has outgrown the tank size or the heating element/burner cannot keep up, upgrading capacity or switching to tankless may be the answer.

## 5. Rising Energy Bills

Older units lose efficiency. New ENERGY STAR models can significantly reduce operating costs.
