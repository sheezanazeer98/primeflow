Your main sewer line carries wastewater from your home to the municipal system or septic tank. When it fails, the problem can get messy fast.

## Multiple Drains Backing Up

If toilets, tubs, and sinks all drain slowly or back up together, the blockage is likely **in the main line**, not a single fixture.

## Gurgling Sounds

Bubbles and gurgling after flushing or draining tubs often mean trapped air from a partial blockage or venting issue.

## Sewage Odors Indoors or in the Yard

A persistent rotten-egg smell may indicate a break, bellied pipe, or dry trap — all warrant professional inspection.

## Lush Green Patches Over the Line

Leaking sewage fertilizes grass unevenly. A sudden green stripe along the buried pipe path is a red flag.

## Foundation Cracks or Soggy Soil

Saturated soil from a leaking sewer line can undermine foundations and create sinkholes.
