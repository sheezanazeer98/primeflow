Weak water pressure is frustrating — and the cause is not always obvious.

## Whole-House vs. Single Fixture

If **only one fixture** is weak, the aerator may be clogged or the supply valve partially closed. If **the whole house** is affected, look at the main supply, pressure regulator, or municipal work in your area.

## Pressure Reducing Valve (PRV)

Homes with a PRV can lose pressure when the valve fails. A plumber can test and adjust or replace it.

## Pipe Corrosion and Scale

Older galvanized or corroded copper pipes restrict flow. **Whole-home repiping** may be the long-term fix if pressure drops worsen over time.

## Hidden Leaks

A leak on the supply side can divert water — pressure drops and your meter may run when nothing is on.

## PrimeFlow Diagnostics

We measure pressure at fixtures, inspect valves, and use leak detection when needed. Do not ignore gradual pressure loss — it often signals a bigger problem.
