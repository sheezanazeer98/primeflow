A garbage disposal is convenient — but it is not a trash can. Misuse leads to jams, burned-out motors, and clogged kitchen drains.

## Do Keep Cold Water Running

Cold water solidifies grease slightly so it can be ground and flushed away. Run water **during** operation and for a few seconds after.

## Do Grind Small Amounts

Feed waste gradually. Large batches of potato peels or pasta starch can form a paste that clogs the trap.

## Don't Put Grease or Oil Down the Drain

Liquid grease cools and coats pipes. Combine with soap scum and food debris for stubborn clogs.

## Don't Grind Bones, Fruit Pits, or Fibrous Vegetables

Hard items dull blades; celery, artichokes, and corn husks wrap around the impeller.

## If the Disposal Hums But Won't Spin

Turn off power at the breaker, use the hex wrench on the bottom of the unit to free the impeller, or press the reset button. If it still fails, call PrimeFlow — we repair and replace all major brands.
