Plumbing emergencies rarely happen at convenient times. Knowing what to do in the first few minutes can limit water damage and keep your family safe.

## 1. Shut Off the Water

Locate your **main water shutoff valve** (often in the basement, crawl space, or near the water meter). Turn it clockwise until it stops. For fixture-specific leaks, use the angle stop under the sink or behind the toilet.

## 2. Turn Off the Water Heater

If you shut off the main supply for an extended period, turn off the water heater to avoid damage — electric units can overheat empty tanks; gas units need the pilot/gas valve addressed per manufacturer guidance.

## 3. Move Valuables and Electronics

Get furniture, rugs, and electronics away from standing water. Take photos for insurance if damage is significant.

## 4. Avoid Electrical Hazards

Do not stand in deep water near outlets or appliances. If water has reached electrical devices, stay clear and call professionals.

## 5. Call PrimeFlow Plumbing — 24/7

Describe the situation clearly: location of the leak, whether water is still flowing, and any sewage odor. We prioritize true emergencies and aim for rapid response across NEPA.
