Both methods clear blockages, but they work differently — and the right choice depends on the type of clog and pipe condition.

## Drain Snaking (Augering)

A flexible cable with a cutting head breaks through blockages and retrieves some debris. It is effective for **localized clogs** — hair, small objects, or light root intrusion. It may leave residue on pipe walls, which can lead to recurring clogs.

## Hydro Jetting

High-pressure water scours the **full interior** of the pipe, removing grease buildup, scale, and heavy root masses. It is ideal for **main sewer lines**, restaurant grease lines, and recurring slow drains after snaking.

## Safety Considerations

Hydro jetting requires a **camera inspection** first. Fragile, collapsed, or severely damaged pipes may not withstand high pressure. We assess pipe condition before jetting.
