Northeastern Pennsylvania winters can drop well below freezing for days at a time. When water inside your pipes freezes, it expands — and that pressure can crack pipes, flood basements, and cause thousands of dollars in damage.

Here is how to reduce your risk and what to do if you suspect a freeze.

## Insulate Exposed Pipes

Pipes in unheated spaces — crawl spaces, attics, garages, and exterior walls — are the most vulnerable. Use foam pipe insulation or heating tape on exposed runs. Seal gaps where cold air enters around pipes.

## Keep Heat On — Even When You Are Away

If you travel during winter, keep your thermostat at **55°F or higher**. Opening cabinet doors under sinks on exterior walls lets warm air reach the pipes.

## Let Faucets Drip During Extreme Cold

A slow trickle through a vulnerable fixture can prevent pressure buildup when a pipe starts to freeze. Focus on faucets farthest from your main water line.

## Know Where Your Main Shutoff Is

If a pipe bursts, shutting off the main water valve quickly limits damage. Make sure everyone in the household knows the location.

## When to Call PrimeFlow Plumbing

If you have no water in winter, frost on visible pipes, or suspect a freeze — call **before** the pipe bursts. We can safely thaw lines and repair damage before it spreads.
