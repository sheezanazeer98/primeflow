Most drain clogs build slowly from grease, hair, soap scum, and food scraps. A few changes in habit can prevent backups and costly service calls.

## In the Kitchen

Never pour cooking oil or grease down the sink — it solidifies in pipes. Wipe pans with a paper towel and dispose of grease in the trash. Use sink strainers to catch food particles.

## In the Bathroom

Install a hair catcher in shower and tub drains. Avoid flushing wipes labeled "flushable" — they do not break down like toilet paper and are a leading cause of sewer line problems.

## Garbage Disposal Best Practices

Run cold water while the disposal runs and for a few seconds after. Avoid fibrous foods (celery, corn husks), bones, and large quantities of starchy foods at once.

## When Drains Slow Down

Address slow drains early. Baking soda and vinegar may help minor buildup, but recurring slow drains often mean a deeper blockage that professional drain cleaning or camera inspection can locate.
