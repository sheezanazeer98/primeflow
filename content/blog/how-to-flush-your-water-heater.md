Over time, minerals and sediment settle at the bottom of your water heater tank. That layer insulates the heating element or burner from the water, forcing the unit to work harder and raising your energy bills.

## How Often to Flush

Most manufacturers recommend **flushing once a year**. Homes with hard water may benefit from twice-yearly service.

## Basic DIY Steps

1. Turn off power (electric) or gas supply to the unit.
2. Connect a garden hose to the drain valve at the bottom of the tank.
3. Run the hose to a floor drain or outside.
4. Open the drain valve and let water flow until it runs clear.
5. Close the valve, refill the tank, and restore power or relight the pilot per manufacturer instructions.

## When to Call a Professional

If the drain valve leaks, sediment blocks flow completely, or your tank is over 10 years old with never been flushed, professional service reduces the risk of damage. We can also inspect the anode rod and pressure relief valve during the same visit.
