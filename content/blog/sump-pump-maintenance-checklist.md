Your sump pump is often the only thing standing between a dry basement and a flooded one. In Northeastern Pennsylvania, spring thaws and summer storms put serious demand on these systems.

## Test the Pump Quarterly

Pour a bucket of water into the sump pit until the float rises. The pump should start automatically and clear the water in under a minute. If it fails to start, hums without pumping, or runs nonstop, schedule service.

## Clean the Pit and Intake Screen

Remove debris, gravel, and sediment that can clog the impeller or float. A clean pit prevents false cycling and motor burnout.

## Check the Discharge Line

Make sure the discharge pipe is clear of ice in winter and vegetation in summer. Water should drain at least 6 feet from the foundation.

## Consider a Battery Backup

Power outages often happen during storms — exactly when you need your pump most. A battery backup system adds a second line of defense.
