Choosing between a traditional tank and a tankless water heater depends on your household size, usage patterns, and budget.

## Tank Water Heaters

**Pros:** Lower upfront cost, simpler installation, familiar technology. **Cons:** Limited hot water volume, standby heat loss, shorter lifespan than tankless (typically 8–12 years).

## Tankless (On-Demand) Water Heaters

**Pros:** Endless hot water when sized correctly, smaller footprint, longer lifespan (often 15–20 years), lower operating cost over time. **Cons:** Higher installation cost, may require gas line or electrical upgrades, "cold water sandwich" effect if not sized properly.

## Sizing Matters

Tankless units must be matched to **peak demand** — simultaneous showers, dishwasher, and laundry. Undersized tankless units frustrate homeowners; oversized tanks waste energy.

## Pennsylvania Climate

Cold incoming groundwater in winter means tankless units work harder. Professional sizing and occasional descaling keep performance strong.
